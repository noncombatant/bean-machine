Bean Machine Help

Searching

Bean Machine’s search feature is fairly simple.

  • james brown will match all items that contain both “james” and “brown”, anywhere in their metadata. This includes substrings, so a hypothetical artist named Jameson Brownington will also match. This also means that a hypothetical record named Brown by the band James would match.
  • "james brown" will match all items that contain the quoted phrase anywhere in their metadata. Jameson Brownington and Brown by James no longer match, although James Brownington still would.
  • You can also prefix terms with - to negate them: "james brown" -popcorn matches all items that contain the phrase anywhere in their metadata, but will exclude items that match “popcorn”.
  • Each item has in its metadata the date it was added to the catalog, in the format YYYY-MM-DD. This means you can search for items that were added at a given time, by searching for e.g. 2018-03-02, 2018-03, or even just 2018-.

Prefetching

When you don’t have really good, always-on connectivity (like when walking or driving in areas with spotty cellular coverage), Bean Machine may pause playback for lack of song data.

To alleviate this problem, Bean Machine will prefetch 3 items from your current search results, and then will fetch 1 more item each time you play one. This way, you should usually have a ‘buffer’ of already-downloaded songs even when you have a connectivity gap.
